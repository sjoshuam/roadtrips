Over the years, I have built many models to provide actionable insights into the human and constructued systems that keep the United States running. One of my life goals is to actually see the nation I routinely examine in my work. Towards that end, I aspire to visit and photograph at least:

  • Capitals: Every state's capital city.
  • States: At least one of each state's most populous non-capital metropolitan areas.
  • Population Centers: Nearly all metropolitan areas with populations greater than one million.
  • Significant Sites: UNESCO-listed noteworthy historical, cultural, or ecological sites.

This information dashboard tracks my progress towards achiving these goals and provides planning information for my future trips. There are three data displays.

  • Goal Progress (top-right panel): These figures depict my progress towards achiving my goals. Use the slider bar to see progress counted separately for each region or in total.
  • Travels and Weather (middle panels): These maps depict my past travels, remaining destinations, and the typical weather in each place during each month.
  • Destination Distances (bottom panels): This dendrogram depicts the distances between cities, grouping nearby cities together for easy reference while planning future trips.

I consider a destination visited and photographed only if I have (a) walked at least one mile in the area and (b) taken at least three architectural photographs. I have visited/photographed BOTTOMLINE of the 120 places on my goal list so far.

Travels and Weather Panel

The map panel on the left depicts my travels and goal destinations, as well as the typical weather conditions in those places. Drag the slider bar to see each of the maps. Hover over destinations (the cities) and routes (lines between cities) to see additional information specific to each map.

  • Travels (Cities): Depicts each of the 120 goal destinations as circles. Circle shading indicates which places I visited and photographed (bright, filled-in), visited long ago without photographing (dim, filled-in), or have not visited at all (very dim, hollow). Hover over each circle to see why it made the list, how many miles I have walked there, and when I last photographed it. Four destinations are outside the contiguous United States. They are shown separately in the panel above.
  • Travels (Routes): Depicts the most recent trip that passed through each visited/photographed place. Hover over the trip lines to see the year, trip name, and destination for that particular trip segment. As the segments show, I have visited many other places along the way to goal destinations.
  • Temperate Weather: The remaining twelve maps score each destination's weather in terms of average temperate hours per day in each month. For this purpose, I define "temperate" as having an ambient temperature between 50°F and 75°F. Brighter, filled-in circles indicate many hours of temperate weather on a typical day. Hover over each circle to see the typical number of temperate hour per day as well as the Koppen climate classification for that region. Weather maps use a different color (rose red) to prevent confusion.

Destination Distances

The dendrogram panel on the right depicts the distances between destinations that I have not yet visited and photographed. Trace the brackets between any pair of places. The horizontal height of the lines between them roughly corresponds to the distance between them. Groups of destinations that all have short-horizontal connections between them are good candidates for future travel routes.

The dots in the middle of each bracket provide information on the group of destinations that the bracket represents. Hover over each dot to see the place that is most central to all places in that group, plus the number of other places in the group. Where space permits, this information is also visible as text beside the dot.

The next version of this figure will be an interactive planning tool. (GitHub Issue #22)
