Years ago, I built my first simulation model of population dynamics in the United States. I got to know US places and population centers very well, and realized how few I had seen in person. I set out on my first roadtrip with little more than a rough destination and boundless curiosity. Since then, I have traveled thousands of miles. These days, I aspire to have visited, photographed and walked around:

  • Capitals: Every state's capital city.
  • States: At least one of each state's most populous non-capital metropolitan areas.
  • Population Centers: Nearly all metropolitan areas with populations greater than one million.
  • Significant Sites: Noteworthy historical, cultural, or ecological sites.

This information dashboard tracks my past progress towards achiving my goals and provides planning information for my future trips. There are three data displays.

  • Goal Progress (top-right panel): Polar barchart depicts my progress towards achiving my goals. Use the slider bar to see that progress counted in terms of cities, state, and regions. Hover over the wedges to see more information.
  • Travels and Weather (middle-left panel below): Map depict my travels so far, remaining unvisited goal destinations, and an assessment of the typical weather in each city during each month. The middle-right panel explains further.
  • Destination Distances (bottom-right panel): Dendrogram depicts the distances between cities, grouping nearby cities together. The bottom-left panel explains further.
